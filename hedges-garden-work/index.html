How It Works

How a garden job works

I'll come and have a look, we'll agree what needs doing, and I'll get on with it. I work by the hour or by the half-day depending on the size of the job, so it's as easy to sort one overgrown border as it is to spend a day getting the whole garden back to where you want it.

Later In The Year

The bigger hedge work belongs in autumn

There's one thing I won't do in summer, and that's take a hedge back hard. Reductions, reshaping an overgrown hedge, cutting into old wood — that work belongs in autumn and winter, once nesting season is done and the plants are heading into dormancy. It suits the hedge and it suits the wildlife, and I'd rather work that way regardless of what the calendar says.

So if you've got a hedge that needs more than a trim, the useful thing to do is get in touch now rather than in October. I'll come and have a look, we'll agree what it needs, and I'll put you in the diary for the autumn — there are only so many workable weeks once the weather turns, and it's a lot easier to plan it in advance than to find a slot at short notice.

Whatever it is in your garden, whether it's something for this month or something for later in the year, get in touch and we'll talk it through.
