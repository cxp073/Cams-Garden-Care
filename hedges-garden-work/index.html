Hedges, Borders & Garden Work

More than just the mowing — mostly through the cooler months.

Lawns are my main thing — but I take on more than the mowing

The lawn is where I put most of my attention, and it's what I'm known for. But a garden is more than its grass, and through the year I take on hedges, shrubs, borders and general garden work too — mainly through the cooler months, when the mowing eases off and there's time to give it the care it needs, and alongside regular work at other times of year when I've got the capacity.

The Work

What I take on

Hedge trimming and shaping, shrub pruning, border tidying and weeding, and general garden tidy-ups — the kind of work that takes a good garden and gets it looking top notch. It's always me doing it, the same person each time, someone who knows the plants and works with them rather than just cutting everything back hard.

Timing

The right time for hedge work is autumn and winter

There's a good reason most hedge and shrub work belongs in the cooler months. Birds nest in hedges through spring and summer, and cutting then risks the nests — so the responsible window for anything more than a light tidy is autumn through late winter, once nesting's done and the plants are heading into dormancy. It suits the plants and it suits the wildlife, which is how I'd rather work anyway.

That's also why it's worth getting in touch ahead of time. If you know your hedges want doing this autumn or winter, a quick message now means I can pencil it in — the cooler-month diary fills up, and there are only so many workable weeks once the weather turns.

If there's something in your garden you've been meaning to get sorted, get in touch and we'll talk it through.
