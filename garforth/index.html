What I do in Garforth

Two flows of work, side by side. The regular cutting round that keeps a lawn looking right through the season, and the seasonal renovation work that keeps it genuinely healthy underneath.

Regular care:

  • Mowing with a sharp blade
  • Strimming and edging every visit
  • Manual weeding as needed
  • Feeding when the lawn calls for it

Seasonal work:

  • Scarifying to lift moss and thatch
  • Aerating to ease compaction
  • Overseeding to thicken thin patches
  • Topdressing to even out levels
  • Nematodes for chafer and leatherjacket
  • Lime where the soil's acidic

Natural methods, no harsh chemicals. The lawns I look after in Garforth get the same considered care as the rest of the round.

A small Garforth patch, properly served

Garforth lawns sit on the same heavy clay soil that runs through this part of West Yorkshire — which is why aerating and scarifying matter more here than people often think. Compaction and thatch are the quiet problems behind most of the "why won't it green up?" lawns I get asked about. The regular round keeps a lawn looking right week to week; the seasonal work is what keeps it healthy underneath.
